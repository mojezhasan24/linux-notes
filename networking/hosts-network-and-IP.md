## 1. Hosts 

   - Definition: Any device that sends or receives traffic (e.g., computers, phones, servers, IoT devices).
   - Roles: Hosts generally act as either clients (initiating requests) or servers (responding to requests). These roles are relative to the specific communication occurring.
   - Concept: A server is simply a computer with specific software installed to handle incoming requests.

## 2. IP Addresses 

   - Definition: The unique identity of a host, required for all internet communication.
   - Structure: Composed of 32 bits, divided into four "octets" (decimal numbers 0-255).
   - Hierarchy: IP addresses are often assigned in a hierarchical manner (e.g., by location or department) via a process called subnetting, allowing for easier identification of where a host is located.

## 3. Networks 

   - Definition: A logical grouping of hosts that require similar connectivity.
   - Function: Automates the sharing of data between devices.
   - Structure: Networks can contain other networks (subnetworks or subnets). The internet itself is simply a vast collection of interconnected networks.
